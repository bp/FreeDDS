Alias Definitions are DDS Definitions that indirectly point back to older Definitions within the search Dictionaries. (NOTE: this is not to be confused the "alias" names used in the opening of datasets.) There are two styles of Alias Definitions: 1.) alias:name=... and 2.) $:name=....

For example if you had the following Definitions in a Dicitionary, the result of DDS scanning for "format" would result in format=usp (search back through two indirect aliases) and scanning for "data" would result in data=testdata. 

fmt=usp
...
in_data=testdata
...
$old_format=fmt
...
alias:data=in_data
...
$format=old_format
